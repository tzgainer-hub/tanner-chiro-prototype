New Patients

Start with a $49 exam. No pressure, no surprises.

Your first visit is a clear-eyed conversation, a hands-on evaluation, a movement screen, and a written treatment plan. If we are not the right answer for what is going on, Dr. Tanner will tell you — and tell you where to go instead.

Three steps. About an hour.

What to Bring

Come prepared. Get more out of the visit.

  • Photo ID and insurance card (if you'd like us to check coverage)
  • A list of current medications and supplements
  • Imaging or reports from prior providers, if you have them (MRI, X-ray, PT notes)
  • Comfortable clothing — we may run a brief movement screen
  • An honest description of what you've already tried
What to Expect

An hour that actually moves the needle.

  • A real history conversation, not a clipboard
  • A hands-on exam — orthopedic, neurological, postural
  • A movement screen relevant to your activity (golf, tennis, running, hiking, daily life)
  • A clear explanation of what's going on — in plain English
  • A written plan: what we recommend, how long, what it costs
  • If treatment that day is appropriate, we'll usually get started
Insurance & Payment

We'll check your coverage before you come in.

The simplest path: call (480) 595-6101 with your insurance card handy and we'll walk you through what's covered and what's out-of-pocket — before you book, not after.

The $49 new patient exam is a flat cash price. Once we know your situation, we can recommend whether to use insurance, cash pricing, or a treatment package depending on what makes sense for the plan we build.

Frequently Asked

Common questions before the first visit.

Will I be adjusted on the first visit?

Sometimes. It depends on the exam findings. Dr. Tanner won't adjust until he understands what's going on — and if something else (decompression, soft tissue, acupuncture, shock wave) is the better first move, that's what we'll start with.

Do I need a referral?

No. You can book directly.

How long until I feel better?

Honest answer: it depends. Some patients feel immediate relief after the first visit. Chronic conditions and complex injuries are usually a 4–8 week arc with a clear milestone schedule.
