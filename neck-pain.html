Neck Pain & Migraines

If your phone, your laptop, or your steering wheel is in front of your face, your neck has spent years compensating. Most chronic neck pain is mechanical, postural, and fixable — but it takes more than an adjustment.

What's Actually Going On

Seven small vertebrae hold up a 10-pound head. Modern life makes them carry 30.

The cervical spine is built to balance the head over the shoulders. Every inch the head shifts forward roughly doubles the load on the muscles and joints at the back of the neck. By the end of a workday, a forward-head posture can produce loads equivalent to carrying a small child.

The most common patterns we see:

  • Forward head posture from desk work and phone use
  • Old whiplash injuries that never fully resolved
  • Tension headaches at the base of the skull (cervicogenic headache)
  • Migraines triggered by upper cervical and trapezius tension
  • Pain that radiates down between the shoulder blades
  • Numbness or tingling into the arm or hand (cervical radiculopathy)
  • Sleep-related stiffness — wrong pillow, wrong position
  • TMJ-related tension that pulls into the neck

Adjusting a tight neck without addressing the postural cause is a short-term win. We treat both.

When To Come In

Signs your neck shouldn't wait.

  • Headaches more than once a week
  • Stiffness so bad you can't check your blind spot when driving
  • Pain that radiates into the shoulder, arm, or hand
  • Numbness, tingling, or weakness in either arm
  • Pain that wakes you at night
  • A history of whiplash, even from years ago, that you've never had properly treated
  • Migraines that haven't responded to medication

If you have sudden severe headache, vision changes, slurred speech, or facial drooping — that's an emergency room visit, not a chiropractor.

How We Treat It

Adjust, release, retrain. In that order.

A typical neck pain plan combines several modalities — the ratio depends on what the exam shows.

Foundation

Cervical & Upper Thoracic Adjustment

Targeted adjustments to the cervical spine and upper thoracic spine. Almost all neck dysfunction has a stuck upper-back component that has to be released too.

Strength & Stability

Personal Training

Deep neck flexor and upper-back strength is the part most patients are missing. We build it gradually so the spine stops compensating for what the muscles should be doing.

For Muscle Lock-Up

Soft Tissue & Massage

Trigger-point work on upper trap, levator scapulae, and suboccipital muscles — the chronic tension producers in the modern neck.

For Disc Involvement

Cervical Decompression

For radiculopathy — pain, numbness, or weakness running into the arm — caused by cervical disc bulge or herniation. Drug-free alternative to escalation.

For Inflammation

Cold Laser Therapy

Useful for acute flare-ups and post-whiplash tissue healing. Reduces inflammation and accelerates recovery.

The Cause Fix

Postural Retraining

Deep neck flexor strengthening, thoracic mobility work, scapular control. This is what stops the pain from coming back next month.

For Migraine Sufferers

Many of our migraine patients reduced their attacks by half — without medication changes.

Cervicogenic migraine — migraine driven by upper cervical and suboccipital tension — responds well to integrated chiropractic, soft-tissue, and rehabilitation care. We won't promise to cure your migraines, but we will tell you within 4–6 weeks whether neck-driven mechanical care is going to help in your case.
